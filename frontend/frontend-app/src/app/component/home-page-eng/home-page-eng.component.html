Choice : Understanding Social Norms

Context

We are constantly required to follow subtle and implicit social rules in our daily decisions. These rules depend on numerous factors, such as age, gender, and many others. Understanding and respecting them is essential for an individual to be accepted within society.

Thus, extracting data and insights on these everyday social norms could provide a valuable computational understanding of human interactions and play a crucial role in the acceptance of artificial agents.

We therefore address a specific yet fundamental question: how can we understand and predict the socially acceptable ways of distributing the key drivers of human social behavior, namely reward, effort and risk ?

In an initial study, we aimed to develop an artificial agent capable of making socially acceptable distributions of these three dimensionsâ€”considered separatelyâ€”between dyads of human individuals or robots

To achieve this, a game was designed(ethicallychoice.alwaysdata.net) allowing us to collect data and train a predictive model of decision-making.

In this study, we seek to go further by exploring how to design an agent capable of handling combinations of these dimensionsâ€”for example, risk and effort; risk and reward; or even reward and effort.
